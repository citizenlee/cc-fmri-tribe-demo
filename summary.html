TRIBE v2 protocol comparison

How does a viewer's brain engage with two Cultivarium lab protocols — APET plastic cutting vs fungal spore harvesting? A two-day exploration using Meta's TRIBE v2 brain-encoder.

▶ Plastic protocol — interactive timelapse brain · video · networks · steps ▶ Spore protocol — interactive timelapse brain · video · networks · steps ▶ Static contrast viewer — 8 brain maps incl. modality attribution

What we built

  1. Inference pipeline on Modal — wrapped TRIBE v2 in a Modal app (tribe_modal.py) that pulls weights from HuggingFace (facebook/tribev2 + the gated meta-llama/Llama-3.2-3B text encoder), runs whisper → Llama → multimodal fusion, and returns per-vertex predicted BOLD on the fsaverage5 cortical mesh (2414 timesteps × 20 484 vertices for plastic, 1853 × 20 484 for spore).
  2. Four runs per protocol:
    • Full V+A (video + extracted audio + whisper-transcribed narration → Llama → predict)
    • Text-only on the Prism protocol description (gTTS → whisper → Llama → predict)
    • Three modality ablations (video zeroed, audio zeroed, text zeroed) for per-vertex attribution
  3. Analysis — Yeo-7 network parcellation, Destrieux atlas hover labels, a cognitive-demand score = (Control + DorsAttn + SalVentAttn) / (Visual + SomMot), modality-attribution maps.
  4. Three viewers hosted at this Modal endpoint: a static contrast viewer, two protocol timelapses with rotatable brain, video sync, network timeseries, narration density, protocol-step list, and a Meta-demo-style Activity / Modality toggle.

Headline finding

Hard to read ≠ hard to do. The two protocols diverge in opposite directions depending on whether you measure brain engagement to the video or to the text description.

By the cognitive-demand score (association-cortex networks divided by primary-sensory networks):

  • Watching (V+A): plastic engages association cortex 1.6483 relative to sensory, spore 1.4316 — plastic +15%. Watching plastic recruits more frontoparietal control (the network for rule-following, working memory, multi-step planning). Plausibly because the Cricut tool / procedure is unfamiliar and requires sustained tracking.
  • Reading (text-only): plastic 1.7496 vs spore 2.5254 — spore +44%. Reading spore demands more comprehension/integration. More abstract terminology and longer step chains.

Modality attribution

For each protocol, we ran TRIBE four times — once with all modalities, then with each modality zeroed in turn. The variance of full − no_X per vertex is how much modality X contributed to the prediction.

Across both protocols, text dominates by ~7× over video and ~20× over audio in median per-vertex contribution. This reflects (a) TRIBE was trained primarily on narrated naturalistic stimuli, and (b) the narration is dense and constantly evolving while raw video/audio features are slower-varying. It does not mean the brain itself listens 7× more to text — it means TRIBE's prediction depends most heavily on its text encoder for these particular videos.

Caveats

Watching ≠ doing. TRIBE predicts the brain of a passive observer. A real practitioner's brain (motor execution, somatosensory feedback) would engage motor cortex far more strongly.
N = 2. A two-protocol comparison is anecdote, not statistics. To make any quantitative difficulty claim you'd want a battery of 5–10 protocols ranging from trivial to complex.
Step timestamps are approximate. Even-distribution across the video duration — not actual whisper-aligned. Reasonable within ~20 s but the real protocol has uneven step durations.
Modality attribution shows what the model uses, not what the brain actually does. TRIBE's fusion transformer has its own learned priors that bias toward text. Real fMRI of viewers watching these videos would weight modalities differently.
